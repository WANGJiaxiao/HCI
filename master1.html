Apply for master program

To learn how to apply for graduate programs at outstanding universities, our admissions team will provide you with professional guidance.

Application Step

1

identify the research direction

Based on your academic background and career goals, choose a suitable research direction and understand the research fields of the relevant supervisors.

2

Communicate with Supervisor

Communicate with potential supervisors to learn about their research projects and laboratory conditions, and discuss your research interests and plans.

3

submit application

Submit the application materials through the online application system to ensure that all materials are complete and meet the project requirements.

4

Interview assessment

Some projects will arrange interviews or professional tests to assess your academic ability and research potential.

5

Admitted

After receiving the admission notice, confirm the intention to enroll, handle the enrollment procedures, and arrange accommodation and registration matters.

Application Requirements

Required Documents

Bachelor's Degree Certificate

Bachelor's degree diploma and transcript, with a GPA typically required to be above 3.0/4.0.

Letters of Recommendation

Typically, 3 academic letters of recommendation are required, with recommenders familiar with your academic abilities and research potential.

Personal Statement/Research Plan

Describe your academic background, research interests, career goals, and why you have chosen this program and supervisor.

Standardized Test Scores

Depending on the major, GRE or GMAT scores may be required, with GMAT often required for business programs.

English Language Proficiency

International students need to provide TOEFL (minimum 90) or IELTS (minimum 6.5) scores.

Other Documents

Academic Papers or Portfolio

Some programs require submission of published papers, research reports, or professional portfolios to demonstrate academic ability.

Interview

Most programs will arrange interviews (online or in-person) to assess your professional knowledge and research potential.

Professional Background Requirements

Some majors may have specific undergraduate background requirements, and applicants from other majors may need to complete prerequisite courses.

Work Experience

Some master's programs (such as MBA) require relevant work experience, typically 2-5 years.

Start Application

It is recommended to start preparing application materials 6-12 months in advance, especially contacting supervisors and preparing research plans, which require sufficient time. Please check the specific program pages for detailed information as different programs may have special requirements.

Frequently Asked Questions

Learn about common questions and answers regarding graduate applications and studies. If you have other questions, please contact our admissions team.

The duration of a master's program at our university is typically 1-2 years. Research-based master's programs usually last for 2 years, while professional master's programs may be 1-1.5 years. Some programs offer an accelerated option, allowing you to complete your studies in a shorter period.

We offer a variety of graduate scholarships, including full scholarships, teaching assistantships, research assistantships, and outstanding student scholarships. Scholarships typically cover tuition fees and provide a living allowance. The competition for scholarships is intense, and they are primarily awarded based on academic performance and research potential.

You can explore the research interests and publications of potential supervisors on the faculty websites. Choose a supervisor whose research aligns with your interests. It is recommended to contact potential supervisors via email before applying, introducing your background and research interests, and inquiring about available positions.

It is possible to change majors during graduate studies, but you must meet the admission requirements of the target major and obtain consent from both the current and target major supervisors. Changing majors across different fields may require you to complete additional courses, potentially extending your study period. It is advisable to communicate with the graduate coordinators of both programs before applying.

Yes, our university's career development center provides a wide range of internship opportunities and career guidance for graduate students. Many professional master's programs include internship credits and have close collaborations with industry partners. Research-based master's students can also participate in corporate R&D projects or research projects at academic institutions during the summer break.
