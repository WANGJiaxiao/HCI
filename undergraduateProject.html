Program Overview

Established in 1985, the Computer Science program at our university has grown over the past 30+ years into one of China's top-tier programs for training future leaders in computing.

Our faculty consists of internationally recognized scholars and industry experts, with over 80% holding PhDs from prestigious universities abroad. We emphasize computational thinking, algorithmic design, and system development, while encouraging interdisciplinary learning.

In recent years, our graduates have maintained an employment rate of over 98%, with average starting salaries ranking among the highest nationwide. Graduates are employed by leading global technology companies, research institutions, and universities.

Curriculum Overview

Our curriculum emphasizes a balanced integration of theory and practice, offering students a comprehensive and in-depth understanding of computer science — from foundational courses to advanced electives.

Introduction to Computer Science

Core Course

Introduces fundamental concepts, historical development, and application areas of computer science, fostering computational thinking.

Credits: 3
Semester 1

Fundamentals of Programming

Core Course

Introduces basic programming concepts, data types, control structures, and functions using Python as the introductory language.

Credits: 4
Semester 1

Data Structures and Algorithms

Core Course

Covers common data structures (linked lists, stacks, queues, trees, graphs) and their algorithmic implementation and analysis.

Credits: 4
Semester 3

Computer Organization and Architecture

Core Course

Studies the components and working principles of computer hardware systems, including CPU, memory, and I/O systems.

Credits: 4
Semester 4

Foundations of Artificial Intelligence

Elective Course

Introduces basic concepts, methods, and applications of AI, including machine learning, neural networks, and natural language processing.

Credits: 3
Semester 5

Operating Systems

Core Course

Explores core principles and implementation techniques of operating systems, including process management, memory management, file systems, and device management.

Credits: 4
Semester 6

Capstone Project in Computer Science

Core Course

Under faculty supervision, students complete an innovative project that develops independent research and problem-solving skills.

Credits: 6
Semester 7-8

Network Security and Cryptography

Elective Course

Covers fundamental principles and technologies of cybersecurity, including encryption algorithms, authentication, intrusion detection, and defense strategies.

Credits: 3

Apply to the Computer Science Program

We welcome passionate, innovative, and motivated students to apply for our Computer Science program. Below are the application requirements and key dates:

Application Requirements

  • High school diploma or equivalent academic qualifications
  • Strong academic performance in STEM subjects, particularly mathematics and physics
  • Proof of English proficiency (e.g., TOEFL, IELTS, or equivalent)
  • Personal statement outlining your interest in computer science and future goals
  • (Optional) Portfolio of programming projects or proof of participation in coding competitions or awards

Important Dates

  • Fall Intake Application Deadline: January 15 each year
  • Spring Intake Application Deadline: September 15 each year
  • Admission Decision Notification: 4–6 weeks after deadline
  • Fall Semester Start: Early September
  • Spring Semester Start: Early February
